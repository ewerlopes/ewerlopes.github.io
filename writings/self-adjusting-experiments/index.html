Experiments on self-adjusting robots for entertainment support in Physically Interactive Robogames

Working in progress.

We use probabilistic graphical models and Bayesian optimization to design robots capable of self-adjusting their behavior towards maximizing human player entertainment in games. In such scenario, a human player physically interact with a mobile omni-directional robot, while stimulated to maintain strong cognitive tasks, like: trajectory planning, attention and spatial reasoning. The effective game difficulty encountered by players is modulated by a three-dimensional continuous design space, supported by a novel representation of gameplay, which considers each user as a mixture of existing player's types learned from experience.
