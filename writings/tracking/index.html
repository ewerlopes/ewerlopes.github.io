An Adaptive Nonparametric Bayesian Approach for User Tracking in Robotic Game Applications

Estimated realease date: March, 2018.

Game applications involving mobile robots are a potential next product for the mass market, while being a challenging and promising ground environment for testing Human-Robot interaction methodologies. In such domain, the ability to detect and model users is one of the precondition of truly intelligent behavior. In this paper, we present an approach that applies adaptive particle filter based on Gaussian process proposal distributions into a novel adaptive nonparametric method for tracking users in a physical game involving an omni-directional mobile robot. In practical experiments, we compare our methodology against linear-Gaussian state space models, such as Kalman filter, as well as against a simple proportional-based control baseline. The results demonstrate the efficacy of our method in mitigating robot locomotion and localization problems while increasing the overall tracking accuracy and the general believability & acceptance of the robotic agent as a rational and engaging game opponent.
