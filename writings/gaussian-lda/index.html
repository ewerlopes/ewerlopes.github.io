A Gaussian-LDA method for human player categorization in Physically Interactive Robogame

Estimated realease date: March, 2018.

In games between humans and robots, such as Physically Interactive Robogames (PIRGs), it is important that the robotic players are able to adapt to the player's behavior, so to improve entertainment. The on-line construction and maintenance of an accurate player's model is crucial. We propose a new variation of the Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) model for quantitative categorization of human gameplay styles. The original text mining LDA model is based on a multinomial distribution of the topics related to the considered texts over a fixed vocabulary. We base our method on a mixture of player types defined as multivariate Gaussian distribution over a continuous multi-channel stream of data coming from the game, including: player's acceleration, human-robot proximity, and performance evolution through game. This approach has been tested on a dataset collected from a real, physical, mobile robot game, where physical activity and in-game action patterns are extracted using a 3-axis accelerometer sensor module and the Microsoft Kinect RGBD sensor. The obtained results suggest that the proposed method is able to provide a robust description for player interaction in PIRGs.
